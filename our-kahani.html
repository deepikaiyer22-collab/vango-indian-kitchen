Our Kahani

How this started

Kahani means story. This is ours.

I'm Deepika. Born in Delhi, raised in Chennai — so depending on who you ask, I'm either a proper North Indian or a proper South Indian. I've stopped trying to pick a side.

I started cooking when I was 8. Nobody sat me down and taught me, really — I just always wanted to be in the kitchen with my mother, stealing tastes off the spoon before she'd let me. Food is my love language. It always has been.

I moved to Munich in 2017. And somewhere between settling in, building a life here, and raising my daughter, there was one thing I kept missing that I couldn't quite replace — a real, honest, home-cooked Indian meal. Not the restaurant version. The one that takes an entire afternoon and makes the whole house smell like someone's mother is in it.

So I started cooking for people. First a workshop at my daughter's school, then a few colleagues at work who wouldn't let it go. And somewhere along the way, the feedback got loud enough, and the requests kept coming, that I couldn't ignore it anymore.

That's really how Vango started. Not a business plan. Just too many people asking when the next one was.

Here's the thing most people outside India don't realize: there isn't really one "Indian food." Every region has its own spices, its own combinations, its own logic. A Punjabi kitchen and a Tamil kitchen have almost nothing in common except that they're both unmistakably Indian. Every spice carries a story about where it's from, and who used it first, and why.

That's what I want to do with you — demystify it, together, one workshop at a time. Not just follow steps, but actually understand why a dish tastes the way it does. So that when you leave, you're not just full. You're taking a recipe home with you. A small piece of India.

— Deepika
